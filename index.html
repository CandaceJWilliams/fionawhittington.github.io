An endeavour on behalf of the universities in Boston to host the largest all-female and femme non-binary hackathon in the world.

What is SheHacks Boston?


A free event, lasting 36 hours, in which over 1,000 female and femme non-binary college and high school students from all over the United States engage in collaborative computer programming to produce a web, mobile, or hardware product from scratch.


The mission of SheHacks Boston is to: empower women and femme nonbinary individuals in computer science to achieve, provide them with opportunities to explore the tech industry in an inspiring, encouraging, and energizing environment, and to create a community of inclusivity within the Boston tech industry.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register for Shehacks?

Registration will open September 12th, 2017 and closes on Janurary 30th, 2018.

Who can attend?

We welcome female and femme non-binary individuals of all skill levels, who have a passion for learning more about technology and are 16 years or older.

Can I apply if I am a beginner?

We welcome beginners and experts alike! There will be plenty of workshops and mentors available to guide you on your 36-hour hacking journey.

What is the cost of this event?

Thanks to our sponsors, this event is free for anyone who would like to attend!

Will food be provided?

Food from Friday night dinner to Sunday morning breakfast will be all on us! That's right: free food!

Where will I sleep?

We all like to plan to pull an all-nighter at a hackthon, but in case ou do get tired, we will provide matresses for you to sleep on in BU classrooms. We can also match you with a BU student, who will provide you a place to shower and sleep.

What should I bring?

Please bring an ID for admission and a laptop to hack on! We will send out a detailed packing list, closer to the hackathon.

Will you be providing travel reimbursements?

We will reimburse you up to $250 if you live more than 10 miles of Boston. We will also provie free buses from New York City, and several other cities based on where we see a concentration of applicants.

How do teams work?

If you do not already have a team or project for the hackathon, do not worry! Our team will match you with other students with interests and skill levels similar to you based off your answers to our application.

What if I have a question that isn't answered on the FAQ?

As long as your question isn't about the meaning of life, our team is happy to answer any questions you may have about SheHacks Boston. To contact our team, email info@shehacks.io with the subject FAQ and one of us will get back to you as soon as we can.
